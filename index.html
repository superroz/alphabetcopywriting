My work

I write, review and edit content. I've been doing it for 20 years.

I ❤️ plain English and making complicated things easier to understand.

I've worked through my own limited company, Alphabet Copywriting, since 2011.

I worked at:

  • the Government Digital Service from April 2014 to February 2022
  • Ubuntu from February 2009 to October 2011

At the Government Digital Service

I worked on GOV.UK, the Digital Identity programme, and the Digital Marketplace programme.

On GOV.UK, I was the content product lead on the GOV.UK accounts team. I:

  • wrote a style guide to ensure content about GOV.UK accounts was clear and consistent
  • ran a naming workshop to help the team understand if it was calling the product the right thing
  • wrote a blog post about the GOV.UK accounts trial

I was the content lead on the Digital Identity programme. I established a strong user-centred design (UCD) community by:

  • introducing regular design crits so designers could get feedback on their work and make it better as a group
  • re-vamping existing monthly UCD meet-ups so they had a different focus each month, for example 'influencing stakeholders' or 'giving and receiving feedback' - this re-energised the group and felt like a better use of everyone's time
  • writing weeknotes to help everyone understand what was happening in different parts of the programme

I was also the content lead on the Digital Marketplace programme, which was made up of a number of teams working to make digital procurement in government quicker and simpler. I:

  • simplified complex legal documentation, reducing a 250-page contract by half, using plain English
  • created design principles for writing legal content so content designers and lawyers could work together on content more effectively
  • ran a pair-writing project with subject experts and lawyers to write clear and accurate guidance to a tight deadline
  • consolidated disparate pieces of guidance so users could find the help they needed in one place

At Ubuntu

I was the creative content lead at Canonical, the company behind Ubuntu. I wrote a style guide to ensure consistency across all communications, worked with the user experience and web teams to design ubuntu.com, and I was responsible for the content quality of all Ubuntu marketing communications.
